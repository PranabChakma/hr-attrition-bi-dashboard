## HR Attrition BI Analytics Dashboard
## Project Overview
This project analyzes HR employee data to identify the key factors influencing employee attrition. The interactive dashboard provides insights into employee turnover patterns based on age, salary, education, job role, gender, and years at the company.

The objective of this project is to help HR teams identify high-risk employee groups and develop retention strategies.

## Dashboard Analysis
## Attrition by Age

Employees aged 26–35 show the highest attrition (116 employees).
Possible reasons:
- Career switching
- Better external opportunities
- Skill growth phase

## Attrition by Salary

Attrition is highest among employees earning below 5K salary (163 employees).

## Insight:
Lower salary levels strongly correlate with employee turnover.

## Attrition by Job Role

## Highest attrition occurs in:

- Laboratory Technician	 62
- Sales Executive	 58
- Research Scientist	47

These roles may involve:
- High workload
- Performance pressure
- Limited career progression

## Attrition by Gender

- Male Attrition: 151
- Female Attrition: 87
This suggests male employees have slightly higher turnover in the organization.

## Attrition by Years at Company

Attrition is highest in the first few years of employment, especially around 0–5 years.

## Insight:
Employee onboarding and early career development programs may need improvement.

## Key Business Insights

- Low salary is the strongest driver of attrition.
- Employees aged 26–35 are the most likely to leave.
- Early-career employees (0–5 years) show higher turnover.
- Technical and sales roles experience higher attrition compared to managerial roles.
- Improving employee satisfaction and compensation could significantly reduce turnover.

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, organizations should:

- Improve salary structure for lower pay bands
- Focus on retention programs for employees aged 26–35
- Strengthen career development plans
- Improve employee engagement and satisfaction
- Introduce early employee retention strategies
